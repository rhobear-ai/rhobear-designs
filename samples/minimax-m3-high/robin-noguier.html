I started writing HTML in 2009, when I was twelve, on my parents' desktop computer. I have been doing it, mostly, ever since. I'm based in Paris and I work as an independent front-end engineer — usually on long, considered engagements with studios and design teams I trust.

I care about typography (the kind that makes you stop scrolling), motion (the kind you don't notice), performance (the kind you feel without measuring), and accessibility (the kind that respects the reader).

I don't do SEO, I don't do paid ads, and I don't run a YouTube channel. I'm happiest at the intersection of design and code, building websites for people who care.

— Robin, Paris, 2025
